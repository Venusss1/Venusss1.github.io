🏫 Ocean University of China

I am Yanzhe Shan (单衍喆), a senior undergraduate student majoring in Data Science and Big Data Technology at Ocean University of China (OUC) (985 university), ranked 1st out of 37 in my program.

I am currently a research intern at the International Center for Machine Learning (ICML), Peking University, supervised by Prof. Wentao Zhang. My research interests span:

  • 🤖 Data-Centric AI (DCAI) — agent trajectory synthesis & automated data annotation
  • 🔍 Multimodal Deep Search — multimodal information retrieval and long-horizon reasoning
  • 🧠 Agentic RL & Post-Training — GRPO/PPO-based reinforcement learning for LLMs
  • 🖼️ Multimodal Large Language Models — vision-language understanding and generation

Research Highlights

I am a core contributor to AgentFlow, an open-source unified framework for synthesizing high-quality agent training data (trajectory, reasoning trace, tool interaction, environment feedback).

My first research work BrowseComp-V³ — a Visual, Vertical, and Verifiable benchmark for multimodal browsing agents — has been accepted to SIGIR 2026 (CCF-A). I independently led the data synthesis, annotation, and verification pipeline for a major sub-domain, and contributed to experimental analysis.

I also hold a concurrent research position at Harbin Institute of Technology (Shenzhen), supervised by Prof. Yangyang Xu, working on RAG-enhanced controllable video generation.

Skills & Tools

I have hands-on experience with 16× A100 / H20 GPU clusters and strong familiarity with key open-source ecosystems: vLLM, DeepSpeed, Verl (AgentLoop + Function Calling), LlamaFactory, ms-swift, and VLMEvalKit. I am proficient in PyTorch, Python scientific computing, and standard ML/RL post-training paradigms (SFT + RL, GRPO, PPO, MidTrain).

I have also competed in MCM/ICM (Meritorious Winner, Top 7%) and the CCF Algorithm Elite Competition (Excellence Award, Top 10%), and previously trained in competitive programming (C/C++, CCPC provincial award).

    Yanzhe Shan and others
    In Proceedings of the 49th International ACM SIGIR Conference on Research and Development in Information Retrieval, 2026
    Core Contributor
    Abs Bib Code

    We present BrowseComp-V³, a multimodal web-search benchmark emphasizing three core properties: Visual (multimodal inputs), Vertical (deep multi-hop reasoning), and Verifiable (sub-process verifiability). We also release OmniSeeker, a general-purpose agentic framework integrating diverse search and perception tools to tackle complex real-world browsing tasks. The author independently led the full data synthesis, annotation, and verification pipeline for a major sub-domain, and contributed to experimental analysis.
